5 Do's and 5 Don'ts for responsible social media use as a college student.
Do'sDon'tsAudit your privacy settings regularly to ensure you know exactly who can see your personal posts and photos.Don't post illegal or policy-violating content, such as underage drinking or illicit substances, even as a joke.Use a professional headshot and a clean bio on platforms like LinkedIn to make a strong first impression on recruiters.Don't engage in "cyber-arguments" or use hate speech. Heated public debates can permanently damage your reputation.Share your accomplishments, such as class projects, volunteer work, or internship milestones, to build a positive personal brand.Don't vent about professors, classmates, or employers. Future employers may view this as a sign of a "difficult" employee.Think before you "Like" or Share. Interaction with controversial or offensive content can be visible to others depending on the platform.Don't share sensitive personal information, such as your home address, student ID number, or current exact location.Network intentionally by following leaders in your field of study and joining professional groups related to your career goals.Don't ignore your digital footprint. Avoid assuming that "deleted" posts are gone forever; screenshots can live indefinitely.

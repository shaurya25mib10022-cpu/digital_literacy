1. Project Overview
This project serves as a comprehensive exploration of both technical engineering challenges and the essential soft skills required in a digital professional environment. The technical core focuses on Traffic Flow Optimization, utilizing AI and Machine Learning principles to reduce urban congestion. Parallelly, the project covers Digital Communication and Cybersecurity, addressing the "human element" of engineering by practicing professional correspondence, identifying cyber threats (Phishing Case Studies), and establishing robust digital hygiene through a 2FA-centric prevention checklist.

2. Module Summaries
Module A: Professional Digital Communication
In this module, I practiced the nuances of professional email etiquette, specifically focusing on academic and corporate contexts. I drafted formal requests for assignment extensions and an internship inquiry addressed to a coordinator. The primary goal was to master a tone that is respectful yet direct, ensuring clarity in high-stakes digital interactions.

Module B: Responsible Social Media & Digital Ethics
I developed a "Do’s and Don’ts" framework for social media use as a college student. This module emphasized the "Billboard Test"—the idea that one should only post content they would be comfortable seeing displayed publicly on campus. I analyzed how poor digital communication, such as the Better.com Zoom terminations, can lead to corporate and reputational collapse.

I also created a 8-point "Stay Safe Online" checklist tailored for Indian students, highlighting the use of 1930 and cybercrime.gov.in.

4. Personal Reflection
The most surprising insight from this research was that cybercrime is often more about "cracking people" than "cracking code." Realizing how easily urgency and fear can bypass technical firewalls has led me to change my personal habits. I have transitioned from SMS-based OTPs to App-based Authentication and implemented a "five-second rule" to inspect URLs before clicking. These tools will help me academically by providing the technical fluency needed for my AI/ML coursework and the professional mindfulness required for my future engineering career.
